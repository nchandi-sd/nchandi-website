About Us

North County Hospitals and Institutions (North County H&I) is a volunteer organization whose mission is to carry the Alcoholics Anonymous (AA) message to those who still suffer and cannot get to an AA meeting.

Our core service is providing AA volunteer panels to treatment and correctional facilities in North San Diego county. Panels are made up of AA volunteers who visit facilities on a weekly basis to share their individual stories of experience, strength and hope.

We are a 501(c)(3) charitable non-profit organization registered as North County Area Institutions Committee (NCAIC). We are funded by AA members through their AA group contributions and by individual contributions made to our Green Can program that is solely used for AA literature to residents in the facilities we serve.

Visit our Orientation area to learn more about how you can be of service.

Organization Structure


Each facility that we take a meeting into has a panel coordinator. This person coordinates the panels and interfaces with the facility. There are five panel leaders at every facility. These people are responsible for organizing their panel. There can be two to five persons on any panel as determined by the panel leader.

Committee Board meetings are held to conduct the business of North County H&I.

Board meetings consist of reports from each of our sub-committee chairpersons. They report on the status of our efforts in each of these areas:

  • Facilities - our service to the residents and the facilities where we take panels.
  • New Members - welcoming new committee members to AA H&I service work.
  • Librarian - distributing AA literature to the residents of the facilities we serve.
  • Technology - using technology to share the message of AA while keeping within the traditions of AA.
  • So Cal H&I - sharing and learning from other H&I committees in our region.
  • Treasurer - maintaining corporate proverty and financial prudence.
